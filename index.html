About Me

I am a physicist and professor specializing in cosmology, numerical simulations, and data-driven approaches to fundamental questions about the universe. My research integrates theoretical physics, statistics, high-performance computing, and modern machine learning techniques to extract insights from complex systems.

I have a strong interest in scalable numerical methods, statistical inference, neural networks, and AI applications in science and beyond. Beyond academic research, I enjoy translating scientific tools into real-world applications—whether in finance, engineering, or data-intensive industries.

Core Competencies

  • Bayesian inference, uncertainty quantification, and statistical modeling.
  • Deep learning, neural networks, transformers, generative models.
  • High-performance computing (MPI, SLURM, GPU acceleration).
  • Scientific programming in Python, Julia, C++, and JAX/Flux.
  • Data visualization, algorithmic optimization.
  • Collaborative research, mentoring, and interdisciplinary teamwork.
